Ohio University Libraries' artists' books collection metadata application profile

Draft version generated 2020-10-09

Introduction

This Metadata Application Profile (MAP) is intended to guide cataloging of the artists' books collection at Ohio University Libraries' Alden Library. The artists' books collection is a collaboration between Ohio University' Fine Arts Library and the Mahn Center for Archives and Special Collections to build a collection of artists' books. Artists' books are works of art that use the form of the book, and often have material, technical, and other attributes that are of interest to researchers but are not accounted for in a standard MARC/RDA or AACR2 bibliographic record. This MAP is intended to provide a consistent way to provide access to these attributes.

Instructions

This MAP takes the form of a checklist with examples.

  1. Catalog the artists' book according to standard bibliographic practice.
  2. Enhance access to the record by ensuring that the MARC fields listed below are filled per their instructions.

Glossary

  • AAT: The Getty Research Institute's Art & Architecture Thesaurus, code aat.
  • LCGFT: the Library of Congress Genre/Form Terms, code lcgft.
  • RBMS Controlled Vocabularies: The ALA's Rare Book and Manuscripts Section's set of six controlled vocabularies:
    • Binding Terms (code rbbin)
    • Genre Terms (code rbgenr)
    • Paper Terms (code rbpap)
    • Printing & Publishing Evidence (code rbpri)
    • Provenance Evidence (code rbprov)
    • Type Evidence (code rbtyp)

Vocabularies

There are sometimes several terms in a vocabulary that might be applied to an attribute of a book. This section indicates what choices have been made in the past so that similar attributes can be grouped under a common heading instead of being divided into several overlapping terms.

Library of Congress Subject Headings (LCSH)

Frequently used LCSH terms and subdivisions include:

  • --Portraits and --Portraits

Library of Congress Genre/Form Terms (LCGFT)

LCGFT headings for common book attributes:

  • Artists' books is required for every book in the artists' books collection.
  • Book objects may be added in addition to Artists' books to help distinguish books that are strongly sculptural.
  • Photobooks is used for books that are primarily books of photographs, often associated with the TR class.
  • Rebuses has been used for visual puns, e.g. an accordion fold book made to look like an accordion.

Art and Architecture Thesaurus (AAT)

AAT frequently has several headings that seem similar but differ in facet (e.g. a process and a material) and it is easy to miss NT. AAT headings for common book attributes:

  • cloth bindings (bookbindings) (there is also pictorial cloth bindings and publishers' cloth bindings) for hardcover books
  • foil stamping is often referenced in publishers' descriptions as foil or stamping
  • wrappers (book covers)

Deprecated AAT headings

  • Instead of book objects for artworks that make use of the book format but do not communicate as books, and bookworks for works the exploit the book form or focus on the fine crafting of the book, use LCGFT Artists' books and LCGFT Book objects (see LCGFT note above).
